Analyzing Trends in Glaucoma

Using a Large Patient Dataset

Tel Aviv University · Coller School of Management
Supervisors: Dr. Moshe Unger & Din Elron · Students: Omri Baum · Amit Bran · Aviel Sodri

Start Presentation

Glaucoma is a leading cause of irreversible blindness; heterogeneous clinical data complicate early detection.

Motivation

Create a reliable, privacy-safe foundation for progression modeling from real hospital datasets (Sheba Medical Center).

Clinical Challenge

Data are heterogeneous, partially labeled, and collected over long follow-ups.

Goal

High-precision filtering to avoid false removals and keep glaucoma-related data.

Collaboration

Work done with ophthalmologists at Sheba Medical Center.

Outcome

A clean, integrated dataset ready for progression modeling.

Research Objectives

Three concrete goals guiding the analytical design.

Filter non-glaucoma OCT with ≥95% precision via PNU learning.
Integrate OCT (MacularCube/OptiDisc) with Perimetry.
Build a reproducible, scalable pipeline.

MacularCube (~160K), OptiDisc (~110K), Perimetry (~14K) — structural + functional view.

Datasets Overview

MacularCube

GCL+IPL thickness, foveal coordinates, scan quality.

OptiDisc

RNFL thickness, Garway-Heath zones, cup-to-disc ratio.

Perimetry

VFI, MD, PSD + location-wise sensitivities; reliability filters applied.

Unified Schema

Patient ID, Eye, Gender, Scan Date, summary metrics — ready to join.

Standardization, leakage-safe imputation, one-hot with MISSING, and reliability checks.

Preprocessing

Drop constant/ultra-sparse columns; standardize demographics.
Derive age_at_scan, scan_year, scan_month.
Perimetry quality: FP ≤15%, FN ≤25%, CENTRAL_24_2_THRESHOLD_TEST.
Median imputation in-fold; one-hot with MISSING category.

PNU classifiers per non-glaucoma clinic category; threshold tuned for precision.

Modeling Framework

PNU Learning

Positive–Negative–Unlabeled for partially labeled data.

Models

Logistic Regression baseline; XGBoost for production.

Design

Precision-first (≥0.95), τ≈0.97; PR-AUC + Recall@τ for monitoring.

Validation

GroupShuffleSplit by patient ID to avoid leakage.

Integration Results

After filtering: 53,022 glaucoma OCT scans retained.
+ OptiDisc: 45,836 structural records.
+ Perimetry: 17,132 multimodal patient-eye records.
Unified schema enables longitudinal analysis and future progression modeling.

~37% of structural dataset preserved as complete tri-modal cases (high confidence).

Discussion & Trade-offs

Precision-first design reduces recall to avoid discarding valid glaucoma scans.
Labels from clinic categories may overlap; thresholds are adjustable with clinicians.
Summary metrics used (not raw OCT volumes) — conservative but interpretable.

Future Directions

Raw OCT DL
CNN/3D/ViT on volumes to boost sensitivity.
Temporal Models
RNN/Transformers for progression trajectories.
Multi-Site Data
Generalize across devices and populations.
Clinical Tuning
Dynamic τ to balance inclusiveness & caution.

Conclusion

We built a robust, reproducible pipeline that filters and integrates ophthalmic data with ≥95% precision, producing 17K high-quality multimodal records for progression modeling.
